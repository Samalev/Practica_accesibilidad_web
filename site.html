The Great Gatsby

Jay Gatsby, the knight who reigns over West Egg, the host of the nights without respite, but also the triumphant marked by the tragic if not an unintended solitude, is the archetype of those twenties that began with Prohibition and ran through gangsterism and organized political corruption. Protagonist of a decade that would culminate in the catastrophe of 1929, his image of splendour does nothing but announce an inevitable drama. Triumph of perpetual youth, brilliance animated by excess, were also the constants of the life of Francis Scott Fitzgerald, who offers us in The Great Gatsby one of his greatest works.

1984

London, 1984: Winston Smith decides to rebel against a totalitarian government that controls every movement of its citizens and punishes even those who commit crimes with thought. Aware of the terrible consequences that dissent can have, Winston joins the ambiguous Brotherhood through the intermediary of leader O'Brien. Little by little, however, our protagonist is realizing that neither the Brotherhood nor O'Brien are what they appear to be, and that rebellion, after all, may be an unattainable goal. For its magnificent analysis of power and the relationships and dependencies it creates in individuals, 1984 is one of the most disturbing and attractive novels of this century.

Dune

On the desert planet Arrakis, water is the most precious commodity and to mourn the dead, the symbol of maximum prodigality. But something makes Arrakis a strategic piece for the interests of the Emperor, the Great Houses and the Guild, the three great powers of the galaxy. Arrakis is the only known origin of the melange, a precious spice and one of the most coveted goods in the universe. Duke Leto Atreides is assigned to rule this inhospitable world, inhabited by the untamed Fremen and monstrous sand worms hundreds of meters long. However, when the family is betrayed, his son and heir, Paul, will embark on a journey to a destination greater than he could ever have dreamed of. Fascinating mix of adventure, mysticism, political intrigue and environmentalism, Dune became, from the moment of its publication, a cult phenomenon and the greatest science-fiction epic of all time.
